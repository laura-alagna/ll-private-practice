What It Actually Is

Not what you've seen on stage.

Clinical hypnosis is a state of focused attention and heightened inner awareness — not unconsciousness, not sleep, and nothing like what happens at a stage show. You remain fully awake, aware, and in control at every moment. Nothing happens to you without your consent. You cannot be made to do or say anything you wouldn't otherwise choose to do or say.

What hypnosis does is help you access a more receptive mental state — one where the mind is less caught up in its usual chatter and more open to working with new ideas, sensations, and perspectives. In that state, evidence-based techniques for managing pain, anxiety, and stress become significantly more effective.

I've been integrating clinical hypnosis into my practice for over 20 years. It isn't a replacement for talk therapy or other evidence-based approaches — it's a powerful complement to them, and the research is clear on that.

Myths vs. Reality

What hypnosis is not.

✗ Myth

You're unconscious or "under" someone's control during hypnosis.

✓ Reality

You're fully awake and aware throughout. Hypnosis is focused attention — you cannot be made to do anything against your will.

✗ Myth

It only works on suggestible or "weak-minded" people.

✓ Reality

Most people are hypnotizable to some degree. Children are often excellent candidates. The ability to focus and use imagination matters more than personality type.

✗ Myth

Hypnosis is a last resort for people who haven't responded to "real" therapy.

✓ Reality

Clinical hypnosis is most effective when integrated alongside evidence-based treatment — it enhances outcomes, not replaces them.

✗ Myth

You'll reveal secrets or say things you don't want to say.

✓ Reality

Hypnosis doesn't impair judgment or memory. You remain in complete control of what you share — there are no truth serums involved.

The Evidence

What the research actually shows.

Pain

Hypnosis significantly reduces acute and chronic pain perception

Meta-analyses in the Annals of Behavioral Medicine and the International Journal of Clinical & Experimental Hypnosis consistently show moderate-to-large effect sizes for pain management.

Anxiety

Hypnotherapy reduces anxiety comparably to other evidence-based interventions

Research from the American Psychological Association shows hypnosis combined with CBT outperforms CBT alone for anxiety disorders in both adults and children.

Children

Children are often more responsive to hypnosis than adults

Pediatric hypnosis has strong evidence for needle phobia, IBS, headaches, and procedure-related anxiety — often reducing the need for pharmacological sedation.

IBS & GI

Gut-directed hypnotherapy is NICE-recommended for IBS

The UK's National Institute for Health and Care Excellence lists gut-directed hypnotherapy as a recommended treatment for irritable bowel syndrome in adults and adolescents.

Itch & Skin

Clinical hypnosis reduces itch severity in atopic dermatitis

Studies show hypnosis can interrupt the itch-scratch cycle in chronic skin conditions — an area of particular interest in Dr. Lombard's clinical and research work.

Sleep

Hypnosis improves sleep quality and duration

A meta-analysis in the Journal of Sleep Research found hypnotic suggestion increased slow-wave sleep by up to 80% and reduced time awake after sleep onset.
