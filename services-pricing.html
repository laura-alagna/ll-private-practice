Adults

Psychotherapy with Adults

For over 25 years I have had the privilege of listening to and learning from individuals and families as they engage in psychotherapy. In my practice I offer both short-term and long-term work, drawing on a bio-psycho-social framework to help adults meet the challenges of life — managing illness and pain, coping with anxiety, navigating loss and family transitions, and working through relationship difficulties.

My approach is integrative. Alongside talk therapy, I may incorporate hypnotherapy and mindfulness practices, because research shows these tools effectively support coping and skill-building for pain and anxiety. My goal is to help you find relief from distress and move toward wellness — gaining insight, developing new coping strategies, and integrating meaningful change into your life.

Children & Adolescents

Therapy with Children and Adolescents

Growing up is an ongoing, individual process — and when a child is struggling emotionally, not adjusting well, in pain, or having difficulty with peers, the whole family feels it. I believe therapy works best when it is tailored to your child's emotional and developmental needs and to the needs of your family as a whole. My work with children and adolescents always involves the child, parent(s), and when relevant, healthcare providers and educators.

Individual Therapy

Tailored to each child's age and needs — including talk therapy, play therapy, hypnotherapy, and expressive arts approaches such as art and sand tray work.

Family & Group Therapy

Family therapy may involve siblings, the full family unit, or focused parent guidance. Group therapy begins with an intake interview and behavior checklist to ensure the right fit.

School & Provider Consultation

By collaborating with educators and physicians, I help provide flexible, individualized care. School observations and assessments may be part of this work.

Pediatric Hypnosis & Biofeedback

Developmentally appropriate self-regulation tools that help children manage pain, anxiety, habits, and health-related distress — embedded within a therapeutic relationship.

Food Allergies, Asthma & Eczema

Coping with Food Allergies, Asthma & Eczema

As chronic yet largely "unseen" conditions, food allergies, asthma, and eczema present a unique psychological challenge — especially for children and teens. I specialize in helping individuals and families manage the anxiety, pain, and stress that come with these diagnoses, using an integrative behavioral health approach grounded in Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) and adapted specifically for this population.

I recognize that the psychosocial stakes shift at key developmental points — first diagnosis, starting school, overnight camp, adolescence, college — and that treatment must account for this. My goal is to help families reduce anxiety, strengthen coping skills, and build resilience: not just surviving their food allergy journey, but thriving. I am a former faculty member at Northwestern's Center for Food Allergy & Asthma Research (CFAAR) and have spoken at Lurie Children's Hospital's Food Allergy Support and Education program.

Clinical Hypnosis

Hypnotherapy

I integrate medical hypnosis, guided imagery, and mindfulness into psychotherapy for both adults and children. These are evidence-based tools — not what you've seen on a stage — and they work most effectively when embedded within a therapeutic relationship.

For adults, hypnotherapy can help with anxiety, depression, pain management, sleep, smoking cessation, weight and behavioral habits, concentration difficulties, and surgery preparation. For children, pediatric hypnosis and biofeedback offer developmentally appropriate strategies for managing pain, anxiety, habits such as bed-wetting or nail-biting, and gastrointestinal issues like functional abdominal pain.
