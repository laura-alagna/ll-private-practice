Thirty years of listening, learning, and helping people heal.

I became a psychologist because I believe that people are more resilient than they often feel in their hardest moments. My job is to help you find that resilience — and to give you the skills to draw on it long after we stop meeting.

My practice is grounded in a biopsychosocial perspective, which just means I look at the whole picture: your physical health, your emotional life, your relationships, and the experiences that shaped you. I work with adults, children, and families facing anxiety, chronic pain, grief, stress-related health concerns, and difficult life transitions.

I'm particularly passionate about mind-body approaches — especially clinical hypnosis and mindfulness — because the research on these tools is compelling, and I've seen firsthand how much they can help. Whether you're dealing with something specific or just feeling stuck, I'd love to talk.

Outside of my private practice, I've spent years in academic research at Northwestern University studying the psychosocial impacts of food allergy, working in school settings, and training the next generation of clinicians. That breadth of experience shapes how I think about every person who walks through my door.

Background & Experience

A Few Things Worth Knowing

PhD, University of Chicago

I completed both my undergraduate and doctoral training at the University of Chicago, earning a PhD in Psychology and Human Development.

10+ Years in Schools

I spent over a decade delivering therapy directly within a preschool and elementary school setting — working with children, guiding parents, supervising trainees, and consulting on trauma and SEL.

Research at Northwestern

As a former Research Assistant Professor at Northwestern University's Feinberg School of Medicine, I studied the psychosocial impact of food allergy and developed self-directed coping tools — leading to multiple peer-reviewed publications.

ASCH Approved Consultant in Hypnosis

I hold the highest credential offered by the American Society of Clinical Hypnosis and have integrated evidence-based hypnotherapy into my clinical work for over two decades.

Leadership in the Field

I currently serve as President of the Board of the National Pediatric Hypnosis Training Institute (NPHTI) and as President-Elect of APA Division 30. I was formerly President of the Chicago Society of Clinical Hypnosis.
